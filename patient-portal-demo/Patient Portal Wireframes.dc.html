Patient Portal — Wireframe Flows
Crawfurd Medical patient portal — PWA wireframes in two views. Journey threads the screens into the end-to-end client experience across the clinic's swimlanes (Client · CA · Reception · Doctor) over the five health-screening phases. Inventory is the screen-by-screen catalogue (10 flows, 45+ frames) — the single source the Journey draws its screens from. Switch in Tweaks ▸ layout; in Inventory use view to isolate a flow. Clients create an account first, then browse, assess & build a selection — booking itself is arranged with our team by phone, email or WhatsApp (no in-app booking).

2
Home Dashboard
— shell direction (A/B) + 3 member states (C–E)
A · Action-first
B · Body-map-first
Member states — Home renders 1 of 4 by status at login
New — choose your path (no assessment yet)
C · Lapsed — complete assessment
D · Active — next step (recs)
E · New report ready (post-screening)
A pushes the next action, B leads with the Body Map. Bottom tab bar is shared across both.
Every client is signed in, so the shell always shows live insight & recommendation cards — there is no locked/guest state.
At login Home picks ONE state: C lapsed → complete assessment; D active → see recommendations → contact the clinic. Members pre-booked by the clinic (account created for them) simply land on the active Home — no appointment or pre-visit form. After a screening, E highlights the new report right on Home; tapping it opens the Health Report.

5
Contact the clinic
— choose a package, then reach us to arrange it · phone · email · WhatsApp
1 · Your selection
2 · Choose how to reach us
3 · Request sent
The app helps you choose a package and build a selection — then hands off to our team to book. Booking, rescheduling and cancelling all happen with us via phone, email or WhatsApp. The app itself never books or manages visits.
An account is required and unlocks the portal (reports, body map, trends). Members pre-booked by the clinic have an account created for them — there is no in-app appointment to manage.
6
After Your Screening
— Home highlights the new report; tap straight through to it
SD1 · Home — new report highlighted
SD2 · Health report
After the in-clinic screening there’s no separate notification or “results ready” screen — the Home dashboard simply highlights the new report on its Latest report card, and tapping it opens the Health Report (Section 8) directly.
No pre-visit form, attendance or check-in in the app — those steps are handled by the clinic. The app only surfaces the finished report.

Body Map + Trends
— reusable map (coverage / status), organ detail, and the Trends hub the Home “Trends” button opens
A · Find tests — coverage
B · Your results — status
16 · Organ detail + CTA

Trends hub. The Home “Trends” quick action now lands on T1 — a dashboard of every tracked marker with mini sparklines & year-on-year deltas. Tap a marker for its full-year chart (T2 lipids, T3 ferritin). Strictly report-derived data.
One map, two jobs. Coverage (finding tests): whole silhouette grey, pick a package and covered areas light up navy — tap any area to add tests à-la-carte. Status (results): only tested areas show, colored Good / Attention. Variant A (Constellation) is the locked mobile lead.
This is the same map that anchors the Journey — both the assessment-first and browse-first paths converge here. Full exploration of A/B/C directions lives in the Body Map canvas.
